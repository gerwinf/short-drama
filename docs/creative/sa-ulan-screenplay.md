# SA ULAN — *(In the Rain)*
### A Kilig short film · vertical 9:16 · ~3 min · Taglish
*First short-film script for Kilig. Premise: Enemies-to-Lovers. Path chosen: mutual sacrifice (puso + twist fused).*

---

## Why this direction (validated)

Both pillars are blockbuster-proven in Philippine cinema:

- **Office rivals / enemies-to-lovers** — *It Takes a Man and a Woman* (2013, one of the highest-grossing PH films ever, ~₱400M+): two exes forced to compete inside the same company. *A Very Special Love* trilogy, *My Lady Boss*, and *Un/Happy For You* (2024, ₱150M in 5 days) confirm the trope still opens big.
- **Self-sacrifice ("pagpapakasakit")** — the defining emotional engine of the teleserye: real love as ultimate selflessness.

**The differentiator:** the martyr trope is usually one-sided (one person suffers in silence — now a tired cliché). This version makes it **mutual and simultaneous**: both rivals secretly threw away the win for each other, the same night, and discover it together in the rain. The "wait, *he did it too?*" reversal is what keeps it smart, not soapy. Lean on the reveal.

## Register note (Taglish)

Urban corporate rom-coms are heavily code-switched. Calibration used here: **banter / office = English-forward Taglish; hugot / kilig peaks = Tagalog** for weight (the way the real films do it).

## Screenplay (v3)

**1 · EXT. MANILA SIDE STREET — NIGHT — RAIN** *(cold open)*
Downpour, neon on wet asphalt. JAZ and RAFA face off, soaked, breathing hard. She clutches a rain-ruined page to her chest.
> **JAZ:** Bakit mo ginawa 'yun?

Freeze on his face — not angry. Wrecked. **SMASH TO BLACK. TITLE: SA ULAN.**
> SUPER: *Tatlong oras ang nakaraan.*

**2 · INT. OFFICE — NIGHT**
Empty floor, one lamp, two desks facing off. JAZ slides a deck across the aisle onto Rafa's desk without looking.
> **RAFA:** *(reading, reluctantly impressed)* ...Okay. This is actually good.
> **JAZ:** I know. Announcement's tomorrow — Team Lead. Isa lang sa atin.
> **RAFA:** And you think it's gonna be you.
> **JAZ:** *(steel)* I know it should be you. Kaya sisiguruhin kong hindi.

Phone buzzes — BARKADA: *Blind date mo 8PM! Wag kang aatras. 😌*

**3 · INT. RESTAURANT — NIGHT**
Candlelight. JAZ dressed up, dry, bracing. A chair scrapes — RAFA. Shock, both faces.
> **RAFA:** ...You?
> **JAZ:** *(menu up like a shield)* I don't know you. Wala akong nakikitang tao.
> **RAFA:** *(matching her)* Agreed. Pagkain lang ang nandito.

*(menus drop; armor drops slower — an hour of unplanned laughter)* His phone lights; he turns away.
> **RAFA:** *(low)* ...I'll fix it, promise. Basta 'wag kang mag-alala.

He returns lighter than he should be. She's watching him differently now. That's the problem.

**4 · INT. OFFICE — LATER, SAME NIGHT**
She doubles back for her charger. On her desk: a manila envelope — an incident report. **Rafa's** mistake, the thing that ends him tomorrow. Her hand hovers. She feeds it into the shredder. No tears — jaw set.
> **JAZ:** *(a dare to the empty room)* Not like this. Tatalunin kita sa pitch — pero hindi sa ganito.

**5 · INT. OFFICE — MORNING — ANNOUNCEMENT**
The floor gathers. HR reads the name — it's **Jaz.** Applause. She's scanning. Rafa's desk is bare.
> **JAZ:** Where's Rafa?

HR slides over a withdrawal letter. Jaz reads the timestamp; the floor drops out.
> SUPER *(on letter)*: *Isinumite: kagabi, 9:47 PM.* — during dinner. Before the report. He **stepped aside** first.

**6 · EXT. STREET — CONTINUOUS**
She runs into the first drops, letter in hand, spots him at the corner walking away.
> **JAZ:** RAFA!

He turns. The sky opens.

**7 · EXT. SIDE STREET — NIGHT — RAIN** *(the cold open, now understood)*
> **JAZ:** Bakit mo ginawa 'yun? Panalo ka na sana.
> **RAFA:** Kasi kagabi, natalo mo na 'ko sa ibang laban.
> **JAZ:** I shredded your report last night. *Ikaw* dapat ang nanalo.
> **RAFA:** ...Anong oras?
> **JAZ:** Bandang alas-nuwebe.

*(the mirror snaps shut — same night, same hour, the same secret choice)*
> **JAZ:** Ang tanga natin.
> **RAFA:** *(cradling her face — hero frame)* Grabe. Magkasing-tanga.

Foreheads together. She laughs through the rain. City glowing behind them.

**8 · TAG — BLACK**
> SUPER: *May mga laban na natatalo mo lang kapag pinanalo mo ang iba.*
> KILIG. *Ikaw ang bida.*

## Shot list — production plan

**Budget reality:** this is the leap from stills to AI **video**, materially pricier per clip than the 1.5-credit stills. Built to de-risk: a 5-shot proof first, then the rest only if it holds. `get_cost` before every batch.

**Standing rules:** 9:16 · faces lock to `kilig-jaz` / `kilig-rafa` · face-free inserts wherever possible · **reuse `enemies.mp4`** for the rain (scenes 1 & 7).
