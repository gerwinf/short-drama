## Problem Statement

Kilig (spec: `Documentation/Product Spec.md`) is a concept for an interactive vertical micro-drama app for the Philippines — teleserye + telenovela + K-drama DNA, Taglish dialogue, branching "Sagot Mo" choices, coins + rewarded ads + ₱99/mo subscription. Founder goal: a $1M ARR company.

The office-hours diagnostic found the spec is a Series A-scope plan sitting on a pre-seed evidence base: demand evidence is market-level only, the founder knows the audience type but cannot currently name a target viewer, and the spec's core differentiator (in-video branching) has a documented failure record in lean-back video. This design reframes the path: buy evidence first, build a social-native studio second, build an app only when a series earns it.

## Demand Evidence

- Current evidence is **market data only** (founder's own words: "Market data only"). No firsthand user contact, no behavioral tests, no named target users.
- What the market data does prove: PH demand for the *passive dubbed* short-drama format is real — DramaBox is the top-grossing entertainment Android app in the Philippines; PH + Indonesia + Mexico are the top 3 short-drama markets by ad-creative volume (Sensor Tower 2026); ReelShort did ~$140M in-app revenue in Q1 2026.
- What it does not prove: that anyone wants an *interactive* or *Taglish-original* version enough to change behavior. These are the two hypotheses this design exists to test.
- Monetization context: 70% of Filipinos prefer free ad-supported viewing; only 20% pay for ad-free (Statista).

## Status Quo

The target viewer's current stack is free and satisfying: TikTok, Facebook video, free teleserye clips, and increasingly DramaBox/ReelShort with rewarded ads. The founder's cultural knowledge (grew up in the Philippines): "masses consume telenovela… today it's also Korean… the population is very influencer driven, celebrities have elite status." The claimed pains (resented coin walls, badly-dubbed content) come from category research, not from users — they are assumptions to verify in the 10 user conversations, not facts to build on.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

- **Target user:** Filipina women 18–34, heavy TikTok/Facebook users, teleserye/K-drama fans; secondary OFW diaspora. Founder currently "knows the type, not a person" — recruiting 10 named, reachable users is part of the assignment, not a given.
- **Narrowest wedge (chosen by founder):** (1) a fake-door ad test — interactive-hook creatives ("tap to decide what she does next") vs passive teasers, measuring CTR and landing-page opt-in; (2) one Taglish pilot story (8–10 episodes) published natively on TikTok/Facebook, using polls as the choice mechanic. No app.

## Constraints

- Founder is not currently in the Philippines; no on-the-ground production capability or current PH user network. PH-based execution (casting, shooting, creator deals) needs a local partner.
- Budget discipline per Premise 1: nothing beyond a few hundred dollars until behavioral data exists.
- Solo founder + AI tooling; no team.

## Premises (agreed)

1. **Evidence first, app later.** Nothing costing more than a few hundred dollars until the fake-door ads and a native pilot produce behavioral data.
2. **Localization is the proven bet; interactivity is the unproven one.** Authentic Taglish content with recognizable faces competes on demand DramaBox already proved. In-video branching is a hypothesis to test cheaply, not the moat.
3. **The pilot lives on TikTok/Facebook, not in an app.** Polls/comments are the choice mechanic; the audience is already there.
4. **(Revised after cross-model challenge)** Vote-the-next-episode is the lead interactivity hypothesis, tested in-pilot **against non-voted control episodes** on completion rate, return viewers, and shares — not vote count. Economically it beats in-video forks because only the winning branch is shot.
5. **Monetization starts free + ad-supported**; coins/Kilig+ wait until retention exists.

**EUREKA (logged):** In-video branching is unclaimed by incumbents not because they can't afford it, but because it doesn't retain in lean-back formats (Bandersnatch/Netflix's dead interactive program; Episode/Choices succeed as *games*, not video). The form of "audience decides" that works at scale in this audience is async social voting. For Kilig: community vote-the-next-episode, which also collapses production cost.

## Cross-Model Perspective

Codex (gpt-5.6, cold read, no conversation access) independently converged on the same wedge:

1. **Steelman:** "The strongest version is not a branching-video app — it's a community-powered IP engine that discovers hit stories cheaply on TikTok/Facebook, then compounds winners into premium serialized franchises."
2. **Most revealing founder answer:** quoted "the population is very influencer driven. celebrities have elite status" — "That points to the actual wedge: recognizable faces and culturally fluent storytelling, not choice mechanics."
3. **Challenged premise 4:** weekly voting may slow narrative momentum and attract low-intent commenters. Resolution: premise revised to include control episodes measured on completion/return/share (founder accepted).
4. **48-hour prototype:** TikTok/FB-native pilot + minimal landing/vote/opt-in funnel with a metrics dashboard (Next.js, Supabase, Cloudflare R2); skip player, branching, coins, subscriptions, auth, native apps.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: The Evidence Sprint (minimal viable)
Two weeks, no product. Fake-door ad test (interactive-hook vs passive-teaser creatives → landing page measuring CTR and opt-in) + 10 recruited conversations with current PH ReelShort/DramaBox users. Kill/continue criteria written before spending. Effort S, risk Low. Con: produces evidence, not a business.

### Approach B: The Social-Native Studio (ideal architecture)
Kilig becomes a TikTok/FB-native drama studio. One 8–10 episode Taglish pilot, weekly drops, vote-the-next-episode via polls with control episodes; only the winning branch is shot. App and monetization deferred until a series proves completion/return/share rates. Effort M, risk Med. Cons: platform dependency; needs a PH production partner; revenue clock deferred.

### Approach C: The Celebrity Co-Owned Show (creative/lateral)
Partner with one mid-tier Filipino influencer (500K–2M followers) as lead and co-producer on rev share; pilot launches on their account; their fanbase is the seed audience. The deal itself is a demand test. Effort M, risk Med-High. Cons: single-creator dependency; deal-making from abroad is the founder's weakest muscle today. Deferred, not rejected — becomes B's casting decision.

## Recommended Approach

**A → B: Evidence Sprint flowing into Social-Native Studio.** A is B's natural first two weeks: the fake-door test answers the interactivity question and the 10 conversations rebuild the founder's user network and verify the claimed pains, before a single episode is shot. B then builds the followable brand asset where the audience already lives, with production cost scaling with evidence. C's creator partnership is revisited at B's casting stage. The original spec's app (player, branching engine, coins, Kilig+, share composer) is **deferred, not deleted** — it becomes the destination if and only if a pilot series earns platform-independent retention.

### Sequence

**Phase 1 — Evidence Sprint (weeks 1–2, extendable to 4 if both contingencies below trigger; budget ≤ $500 + ≤ $200 mixed-result reserve = $700 worst case):**
0. Day-0 checklist, before the sprint clock starts: verify TikTok ad account standing for PH targeting from abroad (payment method accepted, no spend caps, creative review turnaround); stand up the bio-link page — a "coming soon" page carrying the privacy blurb and an optional phone-number field for the superfan/diaspora tail (part of the CC-built instrumented kit: bio-link page, interview script/kit, ad brief with thresholds, tracking spreadsheet — per CEO plan 2026-07-10 and CEO review D4/D5: the opt-in action is a TikTok follow, so per-ad attribution comes from TikTok native reporting, not UTMs); and **reserve the brand surface** — @kilig (or nearest variants) on TikTok/FB/IG plus kilig.ph / kilig.app (~$50–100/yr, outside the ad budget) — before any public ad runs. **Seed the @kilig profile before the first ad impression (CEO review D9):** bio + 3–6 posts (creative variants, character stills, one "ano ang Kilig?" teaser) — the profile is the landing page in the follow-based funnel; an empty profile produces a false kill. **One seed post is a real 60-second Cinderella-revenge micro-episode ending in an end-card choice ("ano'ng gagawin niya? Vote sa comments") — cross-model D11:** its watch-through rate, choice engagement, and organic return are recorded as directional behavioral signal alongside the gate metrics (not gated). **Run a PH trademark screen on "Kilig" before any public spend (cross-model D14)** — handle reservation is not rights protection. Foreign-operated, newly created ad accounts commonly hit review delays — do not let this eat sprint days.
1. Recruit and talk to 10 current short-drama viewers in PH (family network, teleserye/K-drama Facebook fan groups, OFW groups; unpaid, ~20-minute video calls — if recruiting stalls by day 4, offer ₱200–500 load credit per conversation). **Screening rule (cross-model D13): ≥ 7 of 10 must currently watch ReelShort/DramaBox/NetShort or vertical drama clips ≥ 3×/week (one screener question at booking); max 3 recruits from the family chain.** Verify: hours watched, what they pay or refuse to pay, coin-wall resentment (real or assumed?), reaction to "you decide the story."
2. Fake-door ad test on **TikTok only** (single platform keeps the thin budget statistically useful; the target skews TikTok, and Facebook waits for the pilot phase where the OFW/older audience matters). Creative production (per CEO plan 2026-07-10): all variants use ONE story premise — **Cinderella-revenge** (wronged-heroine pagbabalik; CEO review D10) — styled to the spec's design system (§2: Kilig Rose, Fraunces, golden-hour grade) so the test looks like the brand, not default AI output. **AI-generated via Higgsfield — ≥2 interactive-hook ("tap to decide what she does next") and ≥2 passive-teaser variants** (4–6 total), synthetic-media disclosure labeled, one generation pass + at most one quality re-roll per variant (~half a day); **all four variants go live — 2 per category, even budget split (cross-model D12, superseding the top-1-per-category rule)**; the virality predictor demotes to a pre-launch sanity ranking. Category-level comparison is robust to single-creative luck; the CTR gate compares category averages. Both run with a profile-visit/follow objective — the opt-in action is **following the @kilig TikTok account** (per CEO review D4/D5); per-creative attribution comes from TikTok's native per-ad-group reporting. The bio-link page ("Kilig — coming soon") carries an optional number field with **privacy basics (CEO review D7): a consent line, a one-paragraph privacy note (what's collected, why, deletion contact), and a single export-capable form backend** — PH Data Privacy Act applies from the first stored number. Ad account hygiene: 2FA on from day 0. PH targeting. **Launch on day 1–2, in parallel with recruiting** — not after it — so account review delays and impression accrual fit inside the sprint. A celebrity-fronted third creative only if a recognizable face is secured at zero cost during the sprint; otherwise skip — do not delay the test for it.
3. Kill/continue criteria, fixed in the ad brief before launch (defaults; adjust only before spend starts): interactive-category average CTR ≥ 1.5× passive-category average; **absolute floors (cross-model D12): interactive-category CTR ≥ 1% AND cost per net follow ≤ $1**; **follow rate ≥ 20% of ad-driven profile visits AND ≥ 500 total net follows** (recalibrated for the one-tap follow action, CEO review D5); minimum 20k impressions **per category** before reading results. **Measurement-integrity rules (CEO review D8):** the gate reads *paid* per-ad-group metrics only (organic follows logged separately as bonus signal); the gate counts *net* follows (gross and net both recorded); before any gate reading, audit follower quality — sample ~30 followers for real, active PH accounts. If 20k impressions/creative haven't accrued by day 14, extend the read window (up to 1 week) without extending spend — never read early. Conversation gate: **at least 3 of 10 conversations independently confirm the same qualifying pain as an active, unprompted frustration** — qualifying pains are coin-wall resentment and/or dubbing/localization complaints about current short-drama apps. Reaction to the voting concept is scored separately and does not count toward this gate (it informs premise 4, not kill/continue). One offhand comment does not pass.
4. **Daily funnel check (5 min, in the ad brief, per CEO review D6):** campaign delivering? spend pacing to plan? bio-link page loads? Metrics are read ONLY at the pre-registered impression floor — no early peeks. **Creative-rejection fallback:** if a creative is rejected at review (synthetic-media risk), appeal once AND immediately submit the runner-up variant from the same category — the sprint never waits on an appeal. Overbook interviews: recruit 13 to complete 10.
5. **Mixed-result branch:** if the ad test passes but conversations find no pain (or vice versa), do not proceed to Phase 2 on half a signal — extend the weaker track (5 more conversations, or one revised creative round) once, ≤ 1 extra week and ≤ $200. Two mixed reads in a row = kill or re-scope, not a third attempt.

**Phase 2 — Pilot (months 1–3, only if Phase 1 passes; budget ceiling ≤ $500/episode, ≤ $5,000 total including partner fees):**
*Phase 2 gate (cross-model D14): before pilot production starts, this phase must be redesigned against the "Pilot redesign inputs" block in TODOS.md (control methodology, retention measurability, budget realism, vote-to-shoot production timing) using sprint data — and a **rights checklist** (actor likeness, music, script/IP ownership, creator rev-share, platform usage, later app/overseas exploitation) must be signed before any shoot.*
1. Secure a PH production partner or creator (C's option lands here). **Timebox: if no partner is identified within 2 weeks of Phase 1 passing, escalate to the backup channels in Open Question 1 (freelance crews, student crews) or delay Phase 2 rather than raising the budget.**
2. Write one 8–10 episode Taglish story with 2 pre-planned vote points; shoot cheaply; drop weekly on a Kilig-branded TikTok + FB account at a fixed time (8PM, per spec §4.6). Facebook enters here (not in Phase 1) to reach the OFW/older segment.
3. Votes via native polls; control episodes without votes; measure completion, return viewership, shares, follower growth. **Alternate voted and control episodes across the run** (e.g., votes at the ep-3 and ep-7 cliffhangers, controls interleaved) rather than blocking them front/back — the audience grows over the 8–10 weeks, so sequential blocks would confound the voting effect with time. Baseline definition: **each episode's metrics are compared against the trailing 3-episode average of the same metric, recalculated weekly** — not raw totals.
4. **Mid-pilot checkpoint after the first vote point (episode 3–4):** if the voted episode shows no lift over the trailing baseline on any of completion/return/share AND follower growth is flat, stop or re-scope before shooting the back half — do not ride out the full 8–10 weeks on a dead signal.
5. Instrument with native analytics + a spreadsheet first; build the Codex-style dashboard only if the pilot survives.

**Phase 3 — earn the app:** only after a series shows retention that ports off-platform (opt-in list, repeat viewers) does the original spec's app scope re-enter play, starting with the share-card loop (§4.1) — its strongest surviving idea.

## Success Criteria

- **Sprint (2 weeks, worst case 4 with contingencies):** 10 completed conversations with named PH short-drama viewers; ad test live with the pre-registered thresholds above; a written kill/continue decision.
- **Pilot (3 months):** voted episodes beat interleaved control episodes on completion/return/share against the trailing 3-episode baseline; ≥ 5,000 followers on the Kilig account with ≥ 20% of viewers returning for 3+ consecutive weekly drops; at least one episode organically shared beyond the seed audience; total spend within the ≤ $5,000 pilot ceiling.
- **Business (12+ months):** retention that ports off-platform — concretely, ≥ 1,000 opt-ins (phone/email) collected **during the pilot** (the sprint's opt-in action is a TikTok follow; the portable list is built via the bio-link page from pilot viewers) who then engage off-feed (open/click a weekly drop notification) — then ad-supported monetization; $1M ARR is a downstream target that first requires these two gates.

## Distribution Plan

Deliberately platform-native: TikTok and Facebook accounts are the distribution. The only built artifact in Phase 1 is a landing page (any static host; no CI/CD needed). App-store distribution is explicitly deferred to Phase 3. The spec's open question §8.5 (app-store vs web/PWA funnel) is resolved for now: neither — social-native first.
